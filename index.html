Budget Calculator Questions

What is a budget calculator?

A budget calculator is a tool that compares your income with your expenses so you can see how much money is left after your monthly costs.

Can I use this for monthly expenses?

Yes. You can add rent, food, transport, subscriptions, entertainment, savings, other expenses, and your own custom categories.

Can this help me save money?

Yes. After calculating your budget, The Budget Way shows smart suggestions that can help you reduce spending and move money toward savings or important goals.

Is this a South African budget calculator?

The calculator can be used in South Africa and other countries. You can choose your country and currency in the Settings section.

Will my budget disappear if I refresh the page?

No. This version saves your budget entries on your own device using browser storage, so your numbers can stay there when you come back.

Can I download or share my budget results?

Yes. After calculating, you can download a simple text summary or share/copy your results.

What expenses should I include?

Include fixed costs like rent and transport, flexible costs like entertainment, and future-focused categories like savings, emergency funds and debt payments.

Is this financial advice?

No. The Budget Way is an educational planning tool. It can help you understand your numbers, but it does not replace professional financial advice.

Simple Budgeting Tips

A helpful starting point is the 50/30/20 budgeting idea: spend around 50% on needs, 30% on wants, and 20% on savings or debt payments. Your real budget may look different, but this can help you compare where your money is going.

Needs: rent, food, transport, electricity, school fees, medical costs and essential bills.
Wants: entertainment, takeaways, shopping, extra subscriptions and non-essential spending.
Savings: emergency fund, future goals, debt payments, investments or money kept aside.
